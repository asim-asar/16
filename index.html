NBA Salary Project

by Elliott, Asim, Peter, and Caleb



Why do some NBA players make so much money?

Over the past several decades, NBA salaries have gone through the roof. As seen below, the average salary has increased by about 15x since the mid-80s. The globalization of the league and increased sponsorships have led to some superstar players signing yearly deals worth tens of millions of dollars.

In this analysis, we will explore the factors that contribute to how much a player earns in order to better understand what the league values most in its players.



Which NBA player features have the highest correlation with their salaries?


To start our analysis, we first wanted to explore which features are the best indicator of how much a player will be paid. We selected 5 variables that we felt were the best measures of a player's value.

Points Per Game (PPG) had the highest positive correlation with salary (r ≈ 0.73). Value Over Replacement Player (VORP) had the second highest (r ≈ 0.68) and Win Shares (WS) came in third (r ≈ 0.58).

While PPG was the clear winner, all 3 of these variables demonstrated a strong positive correlation with salary and seemed promising to explore.
